🗣️ Daily Practice

Improve your English with small consistent exercises. Here are daily ideas:

  • Write one paragraph about your day.
  • Listen to a short podcast and repeat one sentence aloud.
  • Record yourself reading an English text for 1 minute.
  • Learn 5 new words and use them in a sentence.
  • Translate a short paragraph from English to your language.

💡 Tip: Combine writing and speaking practice daily to build fluency faster.

💬 Expressions of the Week

Discover natural English expressions used by Americans every day:

Break the ice — To start a conversation in a social situation.

Hit the books — To study hard.

Piece of cake — Something very easy to do.

Once in a blue moon — Something that happens rarely.

Speak of the devil — When the person you’re talking about appears.

🧩 Grammar Tips

Learn key grammar differences with examples:

Do vs Make

Use do for actions (do homework) and make for creating (make a cake).

Some vs Any

Use some in positive sentences and any in negatives or questions.

Since vs For

Use since for start points (since 2020) and for for durations (for 2 years).
